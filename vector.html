about the project.


Vector Student Engineering Magazine was my very first creative endeavor and has helped me hone my creative skills. Vector serves as a creative outlet for engineering students, allowing them to explore their aptitudes for writing, photography, and design. In addition, Vector is devoted to covering topics interesting and relevant - early internet communities, personalized ads, robotics clubs - to students at the Cockrell School of Engineering.







my role.


Serving as a co-Layout and Design Editor for Vector, my goal within Vector is to illuminate the art that exists within technology. Working in the areas of layout and graphic design, I strive to make not only beautiful, but functional environments for the incorporated writing and photography. While I mentor the design staff and teach the foundations of layout design, I also develop my own layouts and corresponding graphics.







the challenge.


When designing layouts, it is important to be conscious of the flow in which the reader will observe the content. Through my layout design, I find many of my struggles come from layouts that do not have an obvious flow. As a preemptive measure, I employ UX principles in my layouts - allowing for user error, consistency, functionality, intuitive experience, etc. I find that through establishing basic UX principles as pillars for my work, resulting content receives better feedback from magazine readers.
